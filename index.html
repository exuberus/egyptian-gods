Egyptian Gods

The Egyptian Gods are deities that are part of Ancient Egyptian mythology. These deities, omnipresent and metamorphic, influenced the elements and controlled nature.

The best known cult was that of Isis and Osiris. The Egyptians believed that they populated all of Egypt and instructed the peasants in the techniques of agriculture.

Religion in Ancient Egypt

In Ancient Egypt, religion was polytheistic, that is, several gods were worshiped. These deities had varied roles and characteristics, and were worshiped in Egypt and elsewhere, including in Europe.

The Egyptian gods have much in common with men: they can be born, grow old, and die, besides having a name, feelings, and a body that must be fed.

However, these human aspects hide an exceptional nature: their bodies, made of precious materials, are endowed with a power of transformation, and their tears can originate other beings or minerals.

There are aspects of these gods in various combinations: fully human, fully animal, with the body of a man and the head of an animal, with the entire animal in place of the head (the scarab, for example) or with a human head.

It is interesting to note that there were wars between Egyptian cities that had rival gods.

Egyptian gods and their meanings

  • Ra, God of the Sun
  • Osiris, God of judgment
  • Isis, Goddess of magic
  • Set, God of Chaos and War
  • Anubis, Guardian God
